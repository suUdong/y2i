# 02 CEO Review

## Starting point
- The project already does many things:
- transcript capture
- metadata fallback
- direct ticker extraction
- macro-to-sector-to-stock mapping
- fundamentals enrichment
- master opinions
- cross-video ranking
- signal-date aware backtest
- channel comparison

## The CEO question
- Does this add up to a 10-star product?
- Not yet.
- Right now it is a promising research engine with too many surfaces and not enough product sharpness.

## What a 10-star product would feel like
- A 10-star version would not feel like “run scripts on finance videos.”
- It would feel like:
- “I know which creators are worth my attention.”
- “I know what they really said.”
- “I can see which ideas held up.”
- “I can route those signals into my research or paper-trading stack.”

## The product mistake to avoid
- The mistake would be expanding horizontally across every channel type before nailing one vertical use case.
- That would create a wide but brittle platform.

## Current scope diagnosis
- The current scope is medium-wide technically but still narrow commercially.
- It has a lot of machinery.
- It does not yet have a single crisp user promise.

## 10-star product promise
- A 10-star promise would be:
- “This tool tells me which finance channels generate usable investment signals and turns those signals into a testable research log.”

## What the CEO should prioritize
- Prioritize trust and decision utility over feature breadth.
- The product should answer:
- which channels matter
- which videos matter
- what concrete tradable implications exist
- and whether those implications actually worked

## Best wedge
- The best wedge is not “all finance video.”
- The best wedge is:
- Korean equity / macro / semiconductor creator intelligence

## Why this wedge works
- The content is frequent.
- The audience is obsessive.
- The sectors are economically meaningful.
- The creators have strong narrative influence.
- There is real dissatisfaction with manual tracking.

## Scope expansion vs reduction
- Scope expansion candidate:
- add creator scorecards, portfolio export, notification subscriptions, benchmark-relative performance
- Scope reduction candidate:
- remove generic video analysis and focus only on channel-quality intelligence

## Recommendation
- Selective expansion is the right move.
- Keep the current ingestion/evaluation core.
- Expand only around the product loop:
- ingest
- score
- compare
- alert

## What not to build yet
- Full live trading
- General brokerage integration
- Multi-market universal taxonomy
- Broad social listening
- Creator recommendation feeds for every asset class

## What should be built next
- Creator quality dashboard
- Stable signal taxonomy
- Benchmark-aware backtests
- Confidence tiers for direct vs indirect signals
- Human-review queue for borderline promotions

## 10-star product lens
- A 10-star product is not one with the most capabilities.
- It is one that removes a painful repeated cognitive burden.
- Here, the burden is:
- watching too much finance content
- forgetting what mattered
- failing to test whether the content had signal

## Core moat
- The potential moat is not LLM extraction alone.
- That is commodity.
- The moat is the accumulated structured record:
- creator-by-creator
- sector-by-sector
- signal-by-signal
- with forward outcome tracking

## Product packaging
- Best packaging options:
- subscription analytics dashboard
- research desk companion
- creator intelligence terminal

## Pricing intuition
- A consumer-grade summarizer gets low willingness to pay.
- A creator-signal audit workflow with backtested evidence gets much higher willingness to pay.

## Narrowest initial SKU
- “Track 5 to 20 channels, detect high-signal videos, and produce a creator scorecard with evidence and performance.”

## Expansion path
- Step 1: channel intelligence
- Step 2: personal signal watchlist and alerts
- Step 3: paper-trading integration
- Step 4: collaborative research team workflows

## What I would cut
- I would cut any messaging that implies the system is already a broad autonomous investor.
- That weakens trust.

## What I would emphasize
- repeatability
- auditable evidence
- creator quality scoring
- thesis logs
- signal timing discipline

## Success metric
- Not “how many tickers extracted.”
- Better metrics:
- percentage of actionable videos that users actually open
- retained channels per user
- creator ranking usefulness
- proportion of signals with clear invalidation and follow-through

## CEO verdict
- The product has the right raw ingredients.
- It does not yet have the final product shape.
- The right shape is narrower, more opinionated, and more trust-centric.

## Bottom line
- Do not expand into everything.
- Own one wedge deeply:
- creator signal quality for Korean finance/macro/equity channels
- If you do that well, the product can become 10-star in its niche.
